# Gather Ghana Events — Client Demo Walkthrough

A scripted ~20-minute demo for a prospective organizer (event manager). It is built
around her three stated pain points: **team overwhelm**, **manual bookkeeping**, and
**not being able to track everything**. Lead with those; the rest is dessert.

**Demo URL:** https://gge.ohwpstudios.org · Sign in as `ohwpstudios@gmail.com` (magic link).

---

## Before the demo (10 minutes of prep)

1. **Sign in ahead of time** on the laptop you'll present from (magic links expire in
   30 minutes, so don't get stuck doing email theatre mid-demo).
2. **Seed believable data** so nothing looks empty:
   - 1–2 leads with realistic names (submit the Book form yourself if needed).
   - 3–4 tasks on `/org/tasks` — at least one assigned, one overdue, one done.
   - 4–5 expenses across categories on `/org/books` — mix of planned / committed / paid,
     attached to a lead so margins show.
   - A couple of milestones with escrow amounts on the client page.
3. **Open these tabs in order:** `/org` · `/org/tasks` · `/org/books` · a client page ·
   `/portal` (second browser/incognito, signed in as a test client) · `/e/<slug>` · `/vendors` · `/guide`.
4. **Do NOT demo:** live card/MoMo payment (Paystack live key not yet configured) and
   the Telegram bot (token not set). Speak to them; don't click them.

## The narrative arc

> "You told me three things: your team is overwhelming to coordinate, the books are
> manual, and you're worried things slip through the cracks. Let me show you how this
> platform solves exactly those three — then I'll show you what it does on top."

### Act 1 — "Everything in one place" (Dashboard, 3 min)

Open **/org**.

- Point at the stat row: leads, event pages, contributions raised, escrow held.
- Point at the nav chips: **Tasks (n) · Books · Vendors · Inbox · Team**.
- Scroll to **Recent activity**: *"Every action anyone on your team takes — status
  changes, payments arriving, tasks done — lands here automatically, with who and when.
  This is the end of 'who changed that?'"*

**Line to land:** "This is your whole business on one screen, live."

### Act 2 — Team overwhelm → Tasks (4 min)

Open **/org/tasks**.

- Add a task live: title, due date, assign to a team member, tie it to an event.
- Show the filters: *Mine*, per-person, open/in-progress/done.
- Click the circle to advance one to done; point out the overdue one in red.
- Jump to a client page → the same tasks appear on that event's page.

**Line to land:** "You assign it once, it's on their list, it's on the event, it's
overdue in red if it slips — and the activity trail proves who did what."

### Act 3 — Manual books → Books (5 min)

Open **/org/books**.

- The four cards: **Collected · Outstanding · Costs paid · Projected margin**.
- The per-event table: *"Estimate, what's actually been collected, what the event is
  costing you, and the margin — per event, automatically."*
- Record an expense live (e.g. GH₵ 4,500 catering, committed). Watch totals move.
- Explain the lifecycle: *planned → committed → paid* — "your budget IS your planned
  and committed lines; no separate spreadsheet."
- **Click Export → the CSV downloads.** Open it. *"Month-end for your accountant is
  now one click."*

**Line to land:** "You never reconcile a notebook against MoMo screenshots again."

### Act 4 — "Tracking everything" → the client journey (5 min)

Walk one event end-to-end:

1. **Lead arrives** (Book form → dashboard, with email notifications).
2. **Proposal** — send a quote from the lead row; client accepts in their portal.
3. **Client portal** (switch to the second browser): timeline, proposals, payments,
   escrow — *"your client sees a professional portal, not WhatsApp threads."*
4. **Milestones + the Gather Guarantee**: you mark funded → request release → *the
   client* approves. *"Their money is protected, your professionalism is visible —
   that's why couples say yes to you."*
5. **Messages** — point at the unread badge on the Mensah lead row, open the client
   page, and show the conversation (Ama is asking about a jollof tasting). Reply live;
   it lands in her portal *and* her email. *"No more decisions lost in WhatsApp —
   the conversation lives with the event."*
6. Back on your side: the client page shows money, costs, margin, tasks, messages,
   and the activity trail for that one event.

### Act 5 — The "and more" montage (3 min, fast)

- **/e/ event page** — shareable microsite: RSVP, schedule, gallery, livestream link,
  gift pool. *"Guests abroad see prices in their currency; you settle in GHS."*
- **/vendors** — your curated marketplace; verified badges; reviews.
- **/concierge** — Akwaaba AI plans a starting concept for a couple.
- **/playbooks** — Ghanaian cultural playbooks (knocking, engagement, outdooring).
- **Financing** — "plan now, pay over time" estimates on the Book page.
- **/guide** — the built-in manual, so her team self-onboards.

### Close (1 min)

> "Everything you saw — the tasks, the books, the trail — is live today. Your team
> signs in with just their email, no passwords to manage. We can have your real events
> in here in an afternoon."

Then hand her the capabilities PDF.

## Likely questions & answers

| She asks | You say |
|---|---|
| "Can my team break something?" | Every action is logged with their name; access is revocable in one click from Team. |
| "What about my existing events?" | They're added as leads/clients in minutes; costs can be backfilled per event. |
| "Does it work on her phone?" | Yes — the whole portal is responsive; clients mostly use it on phones. |
| "What does Mobile Money cost?" | Paystack's standard fees; payments reconcile automatically into the books. |
| "What if a client disputes?" | Escrow holds the milestone amount until the client approves — disputes freeze it, nothing leaks. |
| "Can I get my data out?" | One-click CSV exports — events, payments, expenses — anytime. |
